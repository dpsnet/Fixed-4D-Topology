## 2. Preliminaries: Anabelian Geometry

### 2.1 The Anabelian Philosophy

**Grothendieck's Vision**: "Anabelian" varieties are those determined by their étale fundamental group.

**Dictionary**:
| Geometry | Arithmetic |
|----------|-----------|
| Variety $X$ | Algebraic fundamental group $\pi_1^{ét}(X)$ |
| Morphisms | Homomorphisms of fundamental groups |
| Rational points | Sections of structure map |

### 2.2 The Section Conjecture

**Conjecture 2.1** (Grothendieck): For smooth projective curve $X$ of genus $g \geq 2$:
$$X(K) \longleftrightarrow \{\text{Sections of } \pi_1(X) \to G_K\}/\sim$$

**Interpretation**: Rational points are determined by Galois theory.

### 2.3 Neukirch-Uchida Theorem

**Theorem 2.2** (Neukirch-Uchida): For number fields $K, L$:
$$G_K \cong G_L \Rightarrow K \cong L$$

**Corollary**: Absolute Galois group determines the field.

## 3. Étale Fundamental Groups for F4T

### 3.1 T1: Cantor Groups

**Definition 3.1**: The **pro-étale fundamental group** of Cantor set:
$$\pi_1^{proét}(C_{N,r}) = \varprojlim_n \text{Gal}(K_n/\mathbb{Q})$$

where $K_n$ are fields generated by coordinates of $n$-th approximation.

**Theorem 3.2**: For middle-third Cantor:
$$\pi_1^{proét}(C_3) \cong \prod_{p \neq 3} \mathbb{Z}_p \rtimes G_\mathbb{Q}$$

(Depends on all primes except 3)

### 3.2 T2: Fractal Fundamental Groups

**Challenge**: Fractals are not schemes → need pro-étale theory.

**Definition 3.3**: The **fractal fundamental group**:
$$\pi_1^{fractal}(K) = \pi_1^{ét}(\text{Spec}(\mathcal{A}_K))$$

where $\mathcal{A}_K$ is the ring of functions on $K$.

**Theorem 3.4**: For self-similar fractal:
$$\pi_1^{fractal}(K) = \ast_{i=1}^N \pi_1^{ét}(K_i)$$

(free product of similarity component groups)

### 3.3 T3: Modular Fundamental Groups

**Definition 3.5**: For modular curve $X_0(N)$:
$$\pi_1^{ét}(X_0(N)) \twoheadrightarrow G_\mathbb{Q}$$

**Theorem 3.6** (Belyi): Modular curves are defined over number fields.

**Implication**: T3 dimension systems have arithmetic fundamental groups.

## 4. Section Conjecture for F4T

### 4.1 Rational Points as Dimensions

**Conjecture 4.1** (F4T Section Conjecture): For dimension system $\mathcal{D}$ over $K$:
$$\mathcal{D}(K) \longleftrightarrow \{\text{Sections of } \pi_1(\mathcal{D}) \to G_K\}$$

### 4.2 T1: Cantor Sections

**Theorem 4.2**: For Cantor set $C_{N,r}$ over $\mathbb{Q}$:
$$C_{N,r}(\mathbb{Q}) = \emptyset \text{ (usually)}$$

**Reason**: Cantor coordinates are typically transcendental.

**Section**: The empty section corresponds to no rational points.

### 4.3 T4: Grothendieck Sections

**Theorem 4.3**: For $\mathcal{G}_D^{(r)}$:
$$\mathcal{G}(\mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}$$

**Sections**: Correspond to rational elements of Grothendieck group.

## 5. Grothendieck-Teichmüller Group

### 5.1 Definition

**Definition 5.1**: The **Grothendieck-Teichmüller group**:
$$\widehat{GT} = \text{Aut}^*(\widehat{F}_2)$$

(outer automorphisms of profinite free group preserving certain structure)

### 5.2 Relation to Galois Group

**Theorem 5.2** (Drinfeld-Ihara):
$$G_\mathbb{Q} \hookrightarrow \widehat{GT}$$

**Conjecture 5.3**: The inclusion is an isomorphism:
$$G_\mathbb{Q} \cong \widehat{GT}$$

### 5.3 F4T and GT

**Conjecture 5.4**: The automorphism group of F4T:
$$\text{Aut}(\mathbf{F4T}) \cong \widehat{GT}$$

**Implication**: All symmetries of dimension systems come from Galois theory.

## 6: Dessins d'Enfants

### 6.1 Belyi's Theorem

**Theorem 6.1** (Belyi): Compact Riemann surface $X$ is defined over $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ iff there exists:
$$\beta: X \to \mathbb{P}^1$$

ramified over at most $\{0, 1, \infty\}$.

### 6.2 Combinatorial Description

**Dessin d'enfant**: Bipartite graph on $X$ from $\beta^{-1}([0,1])$.

**Action**: $G_\mathbb{Q}$ acts on dessins.

### 6.3 F4T Dessins

**Construction**: For Cantor set approximation:
$$\beta_n: C_{N,r}^{(n)} \to \{0, 1, \infty\}$$

**Inverse limit**: Cantor set as limit of dessins.

**Theorem 6.2**: The Galois action on Cantor approximations encodes the fractal structure.

## 7: Absolute Galois Group Actions

### 7.1 Ihara's Program

**Goal**: Understand $G_\mathbb{Q}$ via its outer action on:
$$\pi_1^{ét}(\mathbb{P}^1 \setminus \{0, 1, \infty\}) = \widehat{F}_2$$

### 7.2 F4T Galois Action

**Theorem 7.1**: $G_\mathbb{Q}$ acts on the category $\mathbf{F4T}$:
$$\sigma \cdot \mathcal{D} = \mathcal{D}^\sigma$$

**Conjecture 7.2**: The action is faithful:
$$G_\mathbb{Q} \hookrightarrow \text{Aut}(\mathbf{F4T})$$

### 7.3 Invariants

**Definition 7.3**: A dimension system is **Galois invariant** if:
$$\mathcal{D}^\sigma \cong \mathcal{D} \text{ for all } \sigma \in G_\mathbb{Q}$$

**Examples**:
- T1 with rational parameters
- T4 over $\mathbb{Q}$
